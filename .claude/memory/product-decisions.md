# Product Decisions Log

Why we built what we built, why we killed what we killed. Read this before proposing features.

---

## BUILT: Trade-specific landing pages (2026-04-13)
**Why**: Generic "small business marketing" doesn't convert contractors. A plumber needs to see plumbing pain points, not generic lead gen talk. Each trade has different seasonality, cost-per-lead, and competitive dynamics.
**Result**: 5 trade pages with deep, trade-specific copy

## BUILT: 5-step audit intake form (2026-04-13)
**Why**: The free audit is the activation moment. Collecting detailed business info upfront means the audit feels personalized, not templated. Multi-step reduces form abandonment vs one giant form.
**Result**: `/audit` page with business details, service area, online presence, current marketing, and collateral upload

## BUILT: Blog with 10 trade-specific posts (2026-04-13)
**Why**: Legitimacy signal. Contractors check if you know your shit before giving you their info. Also SEO play for long-tail keywords like "why your plumbing Google Ads bleed money at 2am."
**Result**: 10 posts, each 400-600 words, targeting specific trade pain points

## KILLED: "Coming Soon" C-Suite agents section
**Why**: Expert panel said it dilutes the current offering. Contractors don't care what's coming — they care what works now. AI Receptionist, CTO, COO, CFO are future product, not marketing material.
**Decision**: Ship when it's real, not when it's a roadmap slide

## KILLED: Dark mode design
**Why**: Design council recommended light mode. Home services = trust, professionalism, clarity. Dark mode felt "too tech startup" for a plumber in Ohio.

## PIVOTED: AI Receptionist → AI CMO
**Why**: User feedback — the original positioning was "AI receptionist taking calls." Real product is an AI CMO that designs campaigns, runs marketing 24/7. Receptionist is a future add-on.
**Impact**: Rewrote entire site messaging around "Fire Your Agency" positioning
